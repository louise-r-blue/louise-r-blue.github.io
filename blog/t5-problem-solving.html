Problem Solving Eloquently

Today one task was to implement a function (like a dish-washer that is built to wash dishes very well & only dishes), that would take in a word/s & determine if a particular letter was present in this word/s - & the total letter count. Then the next step was to implement another function that took in a word & a letter to test it against providing a letter count as well. Well I do not know why but the first function with one tiny change was able to provide for this test. Yet I hammered away trying to find a different approach when it was right in front of me. However the concluding function was an eloquent enough attempt for a novice. I suppose the lesson learned would have to be do not make it any more complicated than need be. Think simple & look at what you have got already.

Functions Described

The map() method is an iterator looping over items & performing an action on each. Given a selection of items - for instance you lay out slices of bread with your aim being to manipulate the slices by providing ingredients & packaging them differently to when they where slices of bread in a plain loaf.

The filter() method as above is an iterator performing an action - in this case a condition on each item & returns only the items that meet the condition. An example would be when you decide to replenish your array of socks. You lay them out inspecting for wear-ability. Your condition is: if none have holes - keep, if any have holes - bin them.

The reduce() method is a mathematical iterator reducing a selection of items to one. I suppose I can mention the making of pizza dough again. You have measured ingredients laid out, & to make a whole ball of dough you methodically add the ingredients till all is one.

Recursion Explained

Ever had a recurring problem & you did not know how to fix it. If you were cycling down a hill & your brakes failed you would be mad (& scared) that you did not attend to the maintenance of your bicycle brakes. Recursion in programming is where a function like I described above with the washing machine but this time the washing machine calls it self again & again - well it does not stop till it explodes. A recursive function calls it self again & again but has (coded) brakes that allows the function to stop processing. Users of the code would be none to happy running a recursive function that did not brake.
