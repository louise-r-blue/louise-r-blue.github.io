Problem Solving Eloquently

Today one task was to implement a function (like a dish-washer that is built to wash dishes very well and only dishes), that would take in a word/s and determine if a particular letter was present in this word/s - and the total letter count. Then the next step was to implement another function that took in a word and a letter to test it against and provide a letter count as well. Well I do not know why but the first function with one tiny change was able to provide for this test. Yet I hammered away trying to find a different approach when it was right in front of me. However the concluding function was an eloquent enough attempt for a novice. I suppose the lesson learned would have to be do not make it any more complicated than need be. Think simple and look at what you have got already.

Reflecting on Confident use of Problem Solving Techniques

Overall I feel fairly confident with my problem solving techniques so far.

  • Psuedocode: I write pigeon english a lot in a scrawl only I can understand.
  • Trying Something: Sometimes a little tentative but fine about trying something
  • Rubber Duck Method: Very cute and will consider such a device.
  • Reading Error Messages:Getting better and if I do not understand I Google.
  • Console Logging: I make good use of this technique
  • Googling: Know what and how to search
  • Asking Peers for Help: Probably not so confident as I feel sometimes I am not sure what I want to ask
  • Asking Coaches for Help: As above
  • Improving processes with Reflection: This I can improve and need to dedicate more time to.
