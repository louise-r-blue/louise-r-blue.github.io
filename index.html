HTML, CSS & the DOM Part.1


Post by Louise R.Blue Nov 2015.

Have you ever received or given a present - lets say a plain cardboard box with a simple message -'To you'. Opening the box you find another plain cardboard box with another message. Open that one & you find two cardboard boxes & so it goes on. This is a lot like html, which forms the basic but fundamental structure of a web page. We need that plain cardboard box.

Your friend or you decide to get some help with css. You receive a colorfully wrapped box with pretty labels, bows & ribbons. Tearing off the paper of you find three more colorfully wrapped boxes with beautiful labels & other goodies attached. The paper is heavily embossed with gold flecks. On opening one of the boxes you find many boxed compartments. One label suggests you go to another box & open it. This goes on & on. How can so many boxes fit in one? That is the wizardry of the DOM. The DOM is like the mother of all trees. Its many branches have many boxes hanging. It has not stopped growing.
